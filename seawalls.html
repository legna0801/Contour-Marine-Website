How We Build

Seawall Construction Process

Three stages. One finished waterfront.

Step 01

Forming & Reinforcement

Formwork is set along the waterline to define the seawall's shape and alignment. Steel rebar cages are then assembled inside the forms, creating a reinforced skeleton that gives the seawall its long-term structural strength. Every cage is tied and spaced to engineered specifications before a single pour of concrete begins.

  • Precision formwork placement
  • Steel rebar cage assembly
  • Engineered spacing & alignment
  • Pre-pour inspection & sign-off
Step 02

Concrete Placement

With the forms set and reinforcement in place, marine-grade concrete is pumped and placed with precision. Our crews work efficiently to ensure a continuous, void-free pour — eliminating weak points that could compromise the structure. The concrete is vibrated and finished to achieve a uniform density throughout the seawall cap.

  • Marine-grade concrete mix
  • Continuous pour — no cold joints
  • Internal vibration for density
  • Surface finishing & curing
Step 03

Finished Seawall

Once cured, the forms are stripped to reveal the finished seawall cap. Final inspections confirm structural integrity and alignment. The result is a clean, durable seawall built to withstand decades of tidal action, boat wakes, and South Florida's storm season — protecting your property and waterfront for the long term.

  • Form removal & final inspection
  • Structural integrity verified
  • Clean finish & alignment check
  • Ready for cap & pilings work
